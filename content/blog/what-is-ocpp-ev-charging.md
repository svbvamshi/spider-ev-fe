## What is OCPP?

Open Charge Point Protocol (OCPP) is an open-source communication standard between EV chargers and central management systems. Think of it as the universal language that lets any OCPP-compliant charger talk to any OCPP-compliant software platform.

## Why OCPP Matters

### Vendor Independence
Without OCPP, you're locked into a single manufacturer's ecosystem. If their software fails or prices increase, you're stuck. OCPP lets you switch management platforms without replacing hardware.

### Interoperability
As charging networks grow, different brands need to work together. OCPP ensures a Tata-manufactured charger can be managed by the same platform as a SpiderEV charger.

### Future-Proofing
OCPP is actively maintained by the Open Charge Alliance with versions evolving to support new features. Investing in OCPP-compliant hardware protects against obsolescence.

## OCPP Versions

### OCPP 1.6J (Most Common)
- JSON/WebSocket based communication
- Supports core charging operations
- Remote start/stop, configuration, firmware updates
- Smart charging profiles
- Widely supported by all major manufacturers

### OCPP 2.0.1 (Latest)
- Enhanced security (TLS, device management)
- ISO 15118 support (Plug & Charge)
- Improved smart charging capabilities
- Better support for energy management
- Display message management

## What to Look for When Buying Chargers

1. **OCPP 1.6J minimum** — ensures compatibility with current platforms
2. **OCPP 2.0.1 ready** — future-proof investment
3. **Certified compliance** — not just "OCPP compatible" marketing
4. **WebSocket connectivity** — more reliable than SOAP-based older versions
