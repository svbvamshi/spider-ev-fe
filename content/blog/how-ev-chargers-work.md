## Understanding EV Charging Technology

Electric vehicle chargers convert grid AC power into the form your EV battery needs. The process varies significantly depending on whether you're using an AC or DC charger.

## AC Charging: The Home Solution

AC chargers supply alternating current to your vehicle's onboard charger, which converts it to DC for the battery. Power levels range from 3.3 kW (single-phase) to 22 kW (three-phase), making them ideal for overnight home charging or workplace top-ups.

### Key Benefits of AC Charging
- Lower installation cost
- Suitable for overnight charging
- Compatible with standard household power supply
- Minimal grid infrastructure requirements

## DC Fast Charging: The Highway Solution

DC chargers bypass your vehicle's onboard charger entirely, supplying direct current straight to the battery. With power levels from 30 kW to 240 kW, they can charge most EVs from 20% to 80% in 20-45 minutes.

### Key Benefits of DC Charging
- Ultra-fast charging speeds
- Ideal for long-distance travel
- Enables fleet and commercial operations
- Reduces EV range anxiety

## Connectors and Standards in India

India primarily uses:
- **Type 2 (AC):** Standard for AC charging up to 22 kW
- **CCS2 (DC):** Combined Charging System for DC fast charging
- **CHAdeMO (DC):** Japanese standard, still common on older EVs

## Choosing the Right Charger

For home use, a 3.3-7.4 kW AC charger handles daily driving needs efficiently. For commercial installations, consider 22-60 kW chargers based on dwell time. Highway corridors demand 120-240 kW DC chargers for rapid turnaround.

## The Role of OCPP

Open Charge Point Protocol (OCPP) ensures your charger communicates with management software regardless of manufacturer. All SpiderEV chargers support OCPP 1.6J for seamless network integration.
