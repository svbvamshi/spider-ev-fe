## Why Start an EV Charging Business in 2026?

India's EV market is growing at 49% CAGR, yet charging infrastructure lags demand. The government targets 400,000+ charging stations by 2030 — creating a massive opportunity for early movers.

## Step 1: Market Research and Site Selection

Location is everything in the charging business. High-traffic areas near highways, malls, office complexes, and residential societies offer the best utilization rates.

### Ideal Site Characteristics
- High vehicle footfall (500+ vehicles/day)
- Adequate electrical infrastructure nearby
- Visibility from main roads
- Sufficient parking space (minimum 2-4 bays)
- Proximity to amenities (cafes, shops)

## Step 2: Understand Regulations and Licensing

The Ministry of Power has de-licensed EV charging, meaning no license is required to set up a charging station. However, you'll need:
- Electrical connection (commercial tariff)
- Local municipal permissions
- Fire safety clearance
- DISCOM approval for load enhancement

## Step 3: Choose Your Charger Mix

A balanced station typically includes:
- 1-2 DC fast chargers (60-120 kW) for quick top-ups
- 2-4 AC chargers (7.4-22 kW) for longer dwell-time charging
- Optional 3.3 kW slow chargers for overnight parking

## Step 4: Investment and ROI

Initial investment ranges from INR 15-50 lakhs depending on charger capacity and quantity. With proper location and pricing, payback periods of 2-4 years are achievable.

## Step 5: Software and Operations

A Charging Point Management System (CPMS) is essential for:
- Remote monitoring and diagnostics
- Payment processing
- User authentication (RFID/app-based)
- Revenue reporting and analytics
- Dynamic pricing capabilities
