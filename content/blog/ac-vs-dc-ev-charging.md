## The Fundamental Difference

The core distinction is simple: AC chargers let your car's onboard charger do the AC-to-DC conversion, while DC chargers handle conversion externally and feed power directly to the battery.

## AC Charging Explained

### How It Works
Grid power (AC) flows through the charger to your vehicle's onboard converter, which transforms it to DC for battery storage. The onboard charger's capacity (typically 3.3-11 kW) becomes the bottleneck.

### Power Levels
- **3.3 kW (Single-phase):** 8-12 hours for a full charge
- **7.4 kW (Single-phase):** 4-6 hours for a full charge
- **22 kW (Three-phase):** 1.5-3 hours for a full charge

### Best Use Cases
- Home overnight charging
- Workplace charging during office hours
- Hotels and restaurants (2-4 hour dwell time)
- Residential societies and apartment complexes

## DC Fast Charging Explained

### How It Works
The charger itself contains powerful rectifiers that convert AC to DC. This high-voltage DC flows directly to the battery, bypassing the vehicle's onboard charger entirely.

### Power Levels
- **30 kW:** 45-60 minutes (20-80%)
- **60 kW:** 25-40 minutes (20-80%)
- **120 kW:** 15-25 minutes (20-80%)
- **240 kW:** 10-15 minutes (20-80%)

### Best Use Cases
- Highway rest stops and fuel stations
- Fleet depot fast turnaround
- Public charging hubs
- Commercial vehicle charging

## Cost Comparison

| Factor | AC Charger | DC Charger |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Hardware Cost | INR 30K - 3L | INR 8L - 50L |
| Installation | Simple | Complex |
| Electricity per kWh | Same | Same |
| Maintenance | Low | Moderate |
| Space Required | Minimal | Moderate |

## The Verdict

For most Indian EV owners, a combination works best: AC charging at home for daily needs, and DC fast charging on highways for long trips. Businesses should assess customer dwell time to choose the right mix.
