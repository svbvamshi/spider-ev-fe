## The Current State of EV Charging in India

As of early 2026, India has approximately 12,000 public charging stations — a significant increase from 2,000 in 2022, but still far short of the estimated 400,000 needed by 2030 to support projected EV adoption.

## Government Policy and Incentives

### FAME II and Beyond
The Faster Adoption and Manufacturing of Electric Vehicles (FAME) scheme has driven infrastructure deployment through subsidies on charging equipment and reduced GST rates.

### State-Level Initiatives
- **Telangana:** 100% subsidy on first 500 public chargers, dedicated EV tariff
- **Andhra Pradesh:** Capital subsidy for charging infrastructure, land allocation
- **Delhi:** Unique EV policy with free land for charging stations

## Key Challenges

### Grid Infrastructure
Many locations lack adequate power supply for high-capacity DC charging. Grid upgrades and transformer installations add time and cost to deployment.

### Land and Real Estate
Urban areas face space constraints. Integrating charging into existing parking infrastructure is critical rather than building standalone stations.

### Standardization
Multiple connector types and payment systems create friction for users. Interoperability mandates are gradually addressing this.

## The Opportunity Ahead

With EV sales growing at 49% CAGR and charging infrastructure at only 3% of the 2030 target, the supply-demand gap represents a massive business opportunity for:
- Real estate owners with parking assets
- Fuel station operators diversifying
- Entrepreneurs entering the EV ecosystem
- Fleet operators building captive infrastructure
